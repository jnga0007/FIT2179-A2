Malaysia on the Map

From global rankings to rural roads, tourism tells the story of who we are.

Data: DTS 2024 · TSA 2024 · World Tourism Organisation Visualisation: Joel Nga Jay El, 2026
01 — Global Position

Malaysia's
Global Stage

Malaysia stands among Southeast Asia's top tourism destinations.

Pre-COVID, we welcomed 35 million international visitors annually. Thailand dominates with 40 million; Singapore matches our numbers.

Tourism generated RM84 billion in revenue before 2019.

Then COVID crashed the industry — borders closed globally.

By 2021, arrivals dropped to just 400,000 visitors.

02 — Domestic Reality

The Domestic
Giant

While international borders closed, Malaysians traveled locally. Domestic tourism is Malaysia's hidden economic giant.

260 million domestic trips in 2024 alone — nearly 7 times larger than international arrivals.

Quarterly numbers show steady post-COVID recovery. Q4 consistently peaks due to year-end holidays.

03 — Geographic Distribution

Where Do
Tourists Go?

Tourism benefits are unequally distributed across Malaysia. Kuala Lumpur and Selangor dominate visitor numbers.

East Malaysia attracts adventurous travelers seeking nature. Rural states struggle to capture tourism dollars.

04 — Following the Money

Following
the Money

Where does tourism money actually flow? Food and beverage services

lead all categories. Accommodation and transport follow closely.

Spending crashed 45% during COVID's peak year.

Recovery exploded in 2022 with 88% growth. By 2024,

expenditure stabilised at healthy rates.

Hotels remain the dominant accommodation choice consistently.

05 — Economic Impact

Jobs and
Recovery

Tourism employs hundreds of thousands across industries. Food services and retail lead job creation.

Employment recovered quickly after COVID restrictions lifted.

Trip duration reveals tourism quality and value. Tourists stayed 2.5 nights average in 2024.

Length of stay has now returned to pre-pandemic levels.
