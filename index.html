01 Global Position

Malaysia's
Global Stage

Malaysia stands among Southeast Asia's top tourism destinations — a position hard-won and harder kept.

Pre-COVID, we welcomed 35 million international visitors annually. Thailand dominates with 40 million; Singapore matches our numbers.

Tourism generated RM84 billion in revenue before 2019.

Then COVID crashed the industry — borders closed globally. By 2021, arrivals dropped to just 400,000 visitors.

35M Peak arrivals (2019)
RM84B Pre-COVID receipts
02 Domestic Reality

The Domestic
Giant

While international borders closed, Malaysians traveled locally — revealing a hidden economic giant.

260 million domestic trips in 2024 alone — nearly 7 times larger than international arrivals.

Quarterly numbers show steady post-COVID recovery. Q4 consistently peaks due to year-end holidays.

260M Domestic trips (2024)
7× Larger than int'l arrivals
03 Geographic Distribution

Where Do
Tourists Go?

Tourism benefits are unequally distributed. Kuala Lumpur and Selangor dominate visitor numbers. East Malaysia attracts adventurous travelers seeking nature. Rural states struggle to capture tourism dollars.

04 Following the Money

Following
the Money

Where does tourism money actually flow? The answer shapes policy, jobs, and livelihoods.

Food and beverage services lead all categories. Accommodation and transport follow closely.

Spending crashed 45% during COVID's peak year. Recovery exploded in 2022 with 88% growth. By 2024, expenditure stabilised at healthy rates. Hotels remain the dominant accommodation choice consistently.

−45% COVID spending drop
+88% 2022 recovery growth
05 Economic Impact

Jobs and
Recovery

Tourism employs hundreds of thousands across industries, providing livelihoods that span the entire economy.

Food services and retail lead job creation. Employment recovered quickly after COVID restrictions lifted.

Trip duration reveals tourism quality. Tourists stayed 2.5 nights average in 2024 — back to pre-pandemic levels.
